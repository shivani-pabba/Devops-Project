Security Roadmap
5-Step roadmap to full E2E verifiability
Section I-A · Paper
1
Independent Verifiability (VMV — This paper)
Selene verifiability layer deployed externally. Not trusted for privacy. Enables voters to verify recorded-as-cast. CES unchanged. ← Current step
2
Casting Encrypted Ballots
Voting front-end collects encrypted ballots directly. CES no longer sees plaintext at vote time. Requires client-side JS or mobile app.
3
Voter Credentials
Generate & store voter key pairs in system. Voters can recover credentials to verify independently without relying on VMV infrastructure.
4
Voter Self-Registration
Voters generate and register their own cryptographic credentials. Risk: disenfranchisement from credential loss. Similar to electronic wallet.
5
Coercion Mitigation
Fake α values, deniable channels. Voters aware & in control of crypto credentials. Full end-to-end security achieved. Civitas-style credentials.
Security properties
Fig. 4 · Paper
✓ Individual Verifiability
Provided when both CES and VMV are honest. Voters can confirm vote recorded-as-cast using tracker.
✓ Universal Verifiability
Anyone can verify NIZKPoK proofs of shuffling and decryption. Tally correctness independently auditable.
⚠ Ballot Privacy
Handled by CES business processes. VMV is NOT trusted for privacy. End-to-end encryption is future work.
✗ Coercion Mitigation
Not implemented in Step 1. Fake α generation and deniable channels reserved for Step 5 of roadmap.
Distributed trust model
Section IV-E
Mix-net: 2 CES + 2 UoS nodes — neither controls threshold alone.
Quorum DLT: 2 CES + 2 UoS nodes — consensus requires 3/4.
Result: "Dishonest but cautious" CES cannot alter election undetectably — detection risk outweighs benefit.
